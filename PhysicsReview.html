17.4 - Electron Volt

The electron volt (eV) is a measure of energy, equal to 1.6 x 10-19 joules.
It is based on the amount of energy an electron acquires by passing through an electric potential of 1 volt.

To find the energy of a particle that traveled over an electric potential, you use the formula:

C x V = Q

Where:

  • C is the charge of the particle, as a multiple of the charge of an electron (1.6 x 10-19 coulombs)
  • V is the potential difference across which the particle travels, in volts
  • Q is the energy the partcle gains, in electron volts
